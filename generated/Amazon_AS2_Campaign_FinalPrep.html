"This was mid-2024 at Capital One. The Risk management organization was spending roughly two months per event on root cause analysis — manually querying databases, correlating signals, writing reports. Everyone knew AI could help, but the company had zero production GenAI in the Risk domain. There was significant skepticism from risk managers about whether an AI system could be trusted with regulatory-sensitive analysis."
T — Task（1句，你的目标）
"I took ownership of building and shipping the company's first production LLM system in Risk — end to end, from architecture to VP-level business case."
A — Action（这是重点，讲你自己做了什么）
"It started in an unexpected way — an intern PM came to me with an urgent PoC request. I had one week. I understood the data, built both a front-end and back-end from scratch, and delivered a working demo on a test dataset. We presented it together to stakeholders, and they were impressed enough to ask me to lead it as both project lead and tech lead. That one-week sprint became an 8-month production build — and it became the first GenAI use case ever deployed in Capital One's Risk domain.

I embedded myself with risk managers early — not to understand the stated problem, but to find where the 2-month bottleneck actually lived. Then I designed the full agent architecture: LLM at the center with RAG grounding in risk policy documents, tool calling for live data retrieval from internal systems, and query rewriting for the vague, context-heavy questions that domain experts ask.

The team grew from 3 to 12 people — I personally onboarded 9 of them, running ramp-up plans, architecture reviews, and coding standards. The hardest challenge wasn't the model — it was eval and trust. I ran a pre-ship audit, found 15% hallucination rate on a representative sample, and pushed back on the ship date. I implemented Reflexion: the model critiques its own output against defined accuracy criteria before returning a response. I also built automated regression suites and a monitoring dashboard for continuous oversight. Hallucination rate dropped 30%.

I also built the business case: ROI projections, VP presentations, framing this as a risk capacity investment rather than an AI experiment. That presentation influenced $10M+ in follow-on funding. We delivered in 8 months — roughly half the time comparable projects normally take. As the system proved its value, we expanded from 1 use case to 7 under the same unified architecture."

"Analysis time went from 2 months to 5 minutes for 100+ risk managers. The system generated $20 million in measured business impact. Seven use cases now run on the same architecture I designed. My VP presentation directly influenced $10M+ in strategic AI investment decisions."

⭐ Story B：MCP Hackathon Multi-Agent — 创新故事【Invent & Simplify · Learn & Be Curious · Think Big】
Invent and SimplifyLearn and Be CuriousThink BigBias for Action
S
"Capital One ran an internal AI hackathon — 21 competing teams, 48 hours. The challenge was open-ended: build an innovative AI application for fraud prevention. The bar was high — the judges had seen every variation of 'chatbot for X' and they were explicitly looking for architectural novelty."
T
"My goal was to build something that felt genuinely new — not just applying the latest model to an existing workflow, but using emerging AI primitives in a novel way."
A
"I decided to build a multi-agent adversarial simulation — attack agents trying to defeat defense agents, so the fraud team could discover attack strategies they hadn't anticipated.

The key architectural decision was using Model Context Protocol — MCP — which Anthropic had just published as an open standard. At the time, most people hadn't used it in production. I used MCP to standardize how each agent accessed its tools: every agent had its own MCP server exposing its capabilities — account query, transaction simulation, detection evasion — and a central orchestrator routed tasks through a unified client interface. This meant I could add or swap agents without rewiring the entire system.

For the attack agents, I used reinforcement learning with a simple reward signal: successfully completing a synthetic attack without triggering the detection system. The agents learned increasingly sophisticated evasion strategies through self-play. I spent roughly 20 hours on architecture and agent coordination, 10 hours on the RL reward shaping, and 8 hours on the demo pipeline."
R
"First place out of 21 teams. The judges specifically cited MCP as a novel and practical approach to agent coordination. The fraud team flagged two attack patterns the simulation discovered as genuinely new threat vectors they hadn't modeled."
Reflection
"The insight was that agent coordination overhead — not model quality — is what kills multi-agent systems at scale. MCP gave us a clean abstraction to iterate fast. I now believe standardized tool protocols will be table stakes for production multi-agent systems within two years."
준비: 如果被追问"什么是MCP？"的一句话回答：
  • "MCP is an open protocol, published by Anthropic, that standardizes how LLMs discover and call tools — think of it as a REST API spec but for agent-tool interaction. The value isn't just convenience; it's that you can swap tools without retraining the model, which matters at scale."
⭐ Story C：CVR 高估诊断 — Deep Dive故事【Dive Deep · Bias for Action · Customer Obsession】
Dive DeepBias for ActionCustomer ObsessionAre Right A Lot
S
"At Rokt, our new campaigns were consistently underperforming in the learning phase — high spend, low conversions. The prevailing explanation was 'learning phase is always noisy' and people had largely accepted it. But the advertiser impact was real: they were burning budget with no return, which is a trust problem, not just a metric problem."
T
"I wanted to determine whether this was random variance or a systematic model failure — and if systematic, find and fix the root cause."
A
"I started the same day without waiting for buy-in. I ran a full EDA across all campaigns in the portfolio, comparing CVR predictions to actual conversion rates across different campaign segments.

Within a day I found a clear pattern: 58% of campaigns had systematic CVR over-prediction. The average bias was +6.5%, but the tail was alarming — some campaigns had predictions 10 times higher than actual. This wasn't noise; it was a structural issue.

I dug deeper into the root cause. The common factor in over-predicted campaigns was conversion type heterogeneity — the same user action was labeled as different conversion types across different advertisers, depending on their individual pixel configurations. The model was learning from inconsistent labels, creating systematic bias for campaigns whose conversion type mix differed from the training distribution.

I designed 12 feature variants to test different standardization approaches. Variant 6 — a cross-advertiser conversion type taxonomy I called 'Lightning Rod' — proved to be the strongest: it mapped heterogeneous advertiser-specific conversion events to a standardized set of seven canonical types, giving the model consistent labels to learn from."
R
"Identified that 58% of active campaigns were affected, with effective CPA inflated 3–5× for those advertisers. The Lightning Rod feature significantly improved CVR calibration in the learning phase. More importantly, the diagnosis reframed the problem from 'expected noise' to 'fixable data quality issue.'"
Reflection
"The lesson: 'the model is working fine on average' is not the same as 'the model is working fine.' I now build systematic calibration audits across subgroups into every model review — average performance is the last thing you should check."
数字备查：
  • 58% of active campaigns affected
  • +6.5% average CVR over-prediction bias
  • 10× worst-case over-prediction
  • CPA inflated 3–5× for affected campaigns
  • 12 feature variants tested
  • V6 "Lightning Rod" = winning variant
⭐ Story D：Strategy 7 生产事故 — Ownership故事【Dive Deep · Ownership · Insist on Highest Standards · Have Backbone】
Dive DeepOwnershipInsist on Highest StandardsHave Backbone
S
"This was a production incident at Rokt. A campaign switched bidding strategies from CPA to ROAS, but the configuration update was incomplete — only one of the two required fields was updated. The pricing system entered an inconsistent state we call 'Strategy 7,' where the system was configured for ROAS but still using CPA-mode quality scoring. Nobody caught it during the update."
T
"I needed to diagnose the revenue impact, find the exact root cause, and ensure this class of failure couldn't happen again."
A
"I started with impact quantification — quality score went from 0.017 to 0.0006, a 97% collapse. Win rate dropped to zero across 12.3 million auctions. Daily revenue was down 62%. This was not a small incident.

I traced the root cause through auction logs in Trino, cross-referencing timestamps across the bidding engine, pricing service, and relevancy scoring systems. I identified the exact field mismatch: 'relevancy_target_roas' had been updated but the 'bidStrategy' enum field had not, creating a contradictory config state that no existing validation layer caught.

Immediate fix was to restore the config to a consistent state. But then I pushed back on stopping there. There was pressure to document the incident and move on — this was a busy period. I argued that this incident revealed a systemic design flaw: our config system allowed invalid states that should be impossible by construction. I proposed and built a validation layer that cross-checks dependent fields before committing any bidding strategy update, making Strategy 7 and similar inconsistent states impossible to enter."
R
"Root cause documented. Validation layer accepted and implemented. No recurrence of this class of incident since. The 12.3M auction impact was contained to a single campaign over a short window."
Reflection
"Production failures are architectural feedback. A hotfix addresses symptoms; a good engineer uses the failure to identify what invariant the system should have enforced. The cost of the deeper fix is always less than the cost of the next incident."
数字备查：
  • Quality score: 0.017 → 0.0006 (97% drop)
  • Win rate: 0% across 12.3M auctions
  • Daily revenue: −62%
  • Root cause: relevancy_target_roas / bidStrategy field mismatch
⭐ Story E：NeMO Vendor → 内部自研 — 【Have Backbone; Disagree & Commit】真实 disagreement with manager 故事
Have Backbone; Disagree & CommitOwnershipDeliver ResultsInvent and Simplify

这是最真实的"disagree with manager"故事。有明确的分歧（继续 vs 放弃项目）、有独立执行（4个月自己做）、有可量化结果（outperform vendor，上线生产）。

S
"At Capital One, our team was working with an external vendor to upgrade one of our production ASR models using NeMO — NVIDIA's speech AI framework. I was the technical point of contact, going deep into their architecture with their engineers. The collaboration was productive. Then leadership decided to terminate the engagement entirely due to data privacy: the vendor's fine-tuning process required uploading customer call data to their external infrastructure, which didn't meet compliance requirements. The project was officially killed."
T
"I believed the underlying technical approach was valuable independent of the vendor relationship. The privacy concern was real, but it was a constraint on deployment, not on the technology. I needed to convince my manager to let me replicate the work internally on our own compliant infrastructure."
A
"I didn't just accept the decision as final. I documented everything I'd learned about the vendor's architecture from our technical sessions, studied our existing codebase to understand what would need to change, and built a specific internal proposal: I separated the two decisions my manager had implicitly bundled together — 'we should drop the vendor' and 'we should drop the approach' — and argued that only the first was justified.

I put together a concrete timeline: roughly 3 months to train an equivalent model on our internal data, 1 month to deploy through our production review process. I presented this to my manager with a clear risk profile — if I failed to hit the benchmarks, we'd have lost 4 months of my time, but we'd have confirmed that the vendor's approach wasn't replicable. That was worth knowing.

My manager was initially skeptical — re-opening a killed project is a hard sell when the team has other priorities. After a few conversations, I got the allocation. I went deep into our codebase, incorporated the NeMO architectural improvements I'd learned from the vendor's technical team, trained the model on our internally-approved dataset, and led it through the full production deployment process."
R
"The in-house model outperformed the vendor's solution on our internal benchmarks. It deployed to production 4 months after I started. We improved ASR model performance, eliminated the vendor cost entirely, and maintained full data privacy compliance. A project that had been officially killed became one of the most impactful model upgrades of that year."
Reflection
"The distinction that matters is: 'not with this vendor' versus 'not with this technology.' Leadership made the right call terminating the vendor — that was a legitimate compliance decision. But when a decision is correct for one reason and incorrect for another, the right response is to isolate which part is wrong and propose a path that addresses only that constraint. Present it as two separate decisions, not one."
关键数字 & 追问准备：
  • 3 months training + 1 month deployment = 4 months total
  • Outperformed vendor on internal benchmarks
  • Deployed to production serving live customer calls
  • Eliminated vendor cost + resolved data privacy concern
如果被追问"你说服了manager，但如果最终失败了呢？"：
"That was part of the proposal — I explicitly scoped the downside: 4 months of my time to confirm whether the approach was replicable. That's a bounded, acceptable risk. I wasn't asking to gamble the team's roadmap, just my own allocation."

"At Rokt, I was building a credit card approval prediction model using 165 features from four data sources — auction signals, user profiles, credit bureau data, and custom-designed features. Science Depth 高频问题 & 回答框架
Q: Walk me through the architecture of an LLM agent system you built in production.
用 Story A 的 Action 部分，加技术细节：
• RAG pipeline: embedding model → vector store → top-K retrieval → context injection
• Tool calling: schema definition → LLM generates JSON → validation → execution → result injection
• ReACT loop: Thought → Action → Observation → repeat until completion or max turns
• Reflexion layer: critique response → identify failures → regenerate with feedback
• Safety guardrails: output classification → escalation trigger → human-in-the-loop for sensitive categories

关键话术："The hardest engineering challenge wasn't the model — it was context management. Tool results accumulate fast across turns. We implemented a summarization step at turn 5 to compress earlier context without losing grounding."
Q: How would you evaluate a multi-step agent? What metrics matter?
不要只说 accuracy。框架：
• Task completion rate (did the agent finish the goal?) — primary metric
• Step efficiency (did it take the minimum necessary tool calls, or did it loop?)
• Tool call precision (did it call the right tool with correct parameters?)
• Hallucination rate (factual accuracy of generated content vs. tool outputs)
• Safety trigger rate (how often does it correctly escalate vs. false trigger?)
• Downstream impact (for advertiser agents: did the recommendation improve campaign performance?)

关键话术："Accuracy on individual steps is a necessary but not sufficient condition. An agent that gets every step right but takes 12 steps to do a 3-step task is not production-ready."
Q: RAG vs fine-tuning for domain adaptation — when do you choose each?
答题框架（Amazon 喜欢 tradeoff 分析）：
• RAG: 知识需要实时更新、知识量大、需要 citation/grounding、数据无法用于训练（机密）
• Fine-tuning: 需要特定输出格式/风格、需要特定 reasoning 模式、latency 要求高（无法 retrieve）、知识相对静态
• 组合: 先 SFT 教模型"如何思考 advertiser 问题"，再 RAG 注入实时 campaign 数据 — 这是我在 Capital One 用的方式

关键话术："For advertiser guidance specifically, I'd use both: fine-tune on historical advertiser interaction data to align tone and reasoning style, then use RAG to inject live campaign stats and auction data. They solve different parts of the adaptation problem."
Q: What are the failure modes you're most concerned about in production agent systems?
展示你的"Tool Failure Taxonomy"（这是你的研究兴趣）：
• Model-side: wrong tool selection, bad parameter generation, hallucinated tool calls, over-calling loops
• System-side: execution failures, stale data, state drift in multi-step workflows

重点放在 state drift："The hardest failure mode isn't a single tool call failing — it's state drift in multi-step execution. Step 3 succeeds but changes the environment in a way that makes step 5's assumption invalid, and the agent doesn't detect the inconsistency. Current retry logic doesn't help with this. It's the agentic equivalent of a transaction isolation problem in databases."

I took that seriously. I researched how senior leaders at Capital One — many with MBA backgrounds — actually made decisions. I studied McKinsey and BCG presentation formats: action titles, pyramid principle, minimum color complexity, leading with the recommendation not the analysis. R: "In the second meeting, she was engaged from the first slide. She commented directly that this presentation was much more effective. BQ 答题通用原则

✅ 一定要做的

  • 每个故事以数字结尾——没有数字就说"directional improvement of ~X%, we didn't have clean measurement because..."
  • 强调你自己做了什么——不是"我们团队"，是"我设计了/我诊断了/我建了"

  • 保持具体和量化——"significant improvement"不够，"58%的活动受影响"才够

最可能的系统设计题
"Design an agentic AI system to help Amazon advertisers optimize their campaigns." (会从更模糊的版本开始)
Step 1：先问澄清问题（花3–5分钟，不要急着设计）
  • "What's the primary channel — conversational UI in ad console, proactive recommendations, or both?"
  • "Which advertiser segment are we prioritizing? SMBs who need more guidance, or sophisticated advertisers who want automation?"
  • "What does 'optimize' mean here — campaign budget allocation, keyword selection, bid adjustments, or all three?"
  • "What's the latency expectation — real-time chat response (<2s) or async workflow (minutes)?"
  • "Are there actions the agent can take autonomously, or is it advisory only?"
  • "What does success look like — advertiser satisfaction, campaign performance improvement, cost reduction?"

Step 2：Frame the problem（after getting answers）
"OK, so I'll design for conversational + proactive recommendations for SMB advertisers, focused on bid optimization and keyword recommendations, with the agent being advisory but able to queue actions for one-click confirmation. The success metric is recommendation acceptance rate and downstream ROAS improvement. Let me walk through the three architectural layers."

Step 3：三层架构（对应 JD 的技术栈）
  • Foundation (Model)：Base LLM + SFT on advertiser interaction data → instruction-tune for "advertiser assistant" persona → DPO from implicit feedback (advertiser applies/ignores recommendation)
  • Capability (Tools via MCP)：auction_simulator, keyword_suggestion_api, campaign_analytics, competitive_benchmark, budget_optimizer. Key: MCP standardizes tool access so we can add tools without retraining.
  • Orchestration：ReACT for conversational single-step queries. For complex multi-step planning (e.g., "restructure my entire campaign"), use planner-executor: planner generates a step list, executor runs each step with human confirmation gates for irreversible actions.

Step 4：强调 Eval + Safety（这是你的差异化）
"Evaluation is where I'd spend disproportionate time. Task completion rate is the north star, but I'd also track tool call precision — did the agent call the right tools? — and downstream campaign performance on a 2-week lag. For safety: advertiser actions are often irreversible — a bid increase that runs for a week can't be undone. I'd implement a confirmation gate for any action above a threshold impact, and a dry-run mode so advertisers can see the predicted outcome before committing."

Step 5：Trade-offs to proactively mention
  • "Personalization vs consistency: a fully personalized agent is hard to audit; a consistent agent may miss individual advertiser context. I'd start with consistent behavior and add personalization layers incrementally."
  • "Latency vs accuracy: synchronous tool calls increase response time. I'd pre-fetch likely tool outputs when the advertiser opens the console."
  • "Autonomy vs trust: the right default for new advertisers is advisory-only; for established advertisers who have accepted many recommendations, we can gradually expand autonomous actions with explicit opt-in."

⑪ 技术速查 — DPO · MCP · ReACT（背下来，可能被直接问）
DPO vs PPO — 一句话对比（面试必考）
PPO（3-step RLHF）
  • SFT → reward model → PPO policy update
  • 需要同时维护 4 个模型（actor, critic, RM, reference）
  • On-policy，能做 online exploration
  • 训练不稳定，计算贵
面试一句话：
"PPO is on-policy RL — powerful but expensive, needs 4 models in memory, and prone to reward hacking."
DPO（1-step）
  • 直接从 (prompt, chosen, rejected) 优化 policy
  • 只需 2 个模型（policy + reference）
  • 离线训练，稳定，数据效率高
  • 对 preference 数据质量极度敏感
面试一句话：
"DPO collapses three steps into one contrastive loss — simpler and stable, but offline, so it can't explore beyond the training distribution."
如果被问到 β 的含义（DPO 的 β 参数）：
"β controls how far the fine-tuned policy can deviate from the reference model. High β = more conservative, stays close to SFT checkpoint. Low β = more aggressive alignment, but risk of distribution collapse. Typical values are 0.1 to 0.5."
ReACT Agent 工作原理
Loop until task_complete or max_turns:
  Thought: "I need to get campaign stats for advertiser 123 to check ROAS"
  Action: get_campaign_stats(advertiser_id=123, metric="roas", period="7d")
  Observation: { "roas": 1.2, "target_roas": 3.0, "spend": 5000 }
  Thought: "ROAS is 1.2 vs target 3.0 — underbidding. I should check keyword CPC."
  Action: get_keyword_performance(...)
MCP 架构（3 要素）

MCP Server（工具侧）

  • 暴露 Tools（可调用函数）
  • 暴露 Resources（可读数据，如 campaign 历史）
  • 通过 list_tools() 让 client 发现

MCP Client（Agent 侧）

  • 连接 server，拉取工具列表
  • 把工具注入 LLM context
  • 把 LLM 的 tool call 路由到正确 server
"MCP 的核心价值不是让 tool call 更容易，而是工具生态标准化——任何 MCP-compatible 工具都可以插入任何 MCP-compatible agent，不需要重新训练。对 Amazon 的广告工具生态（auction API, keyword API, analytics API）非常有价值。"
弱点预案 — DPO 没有生产经验
❌ 不要说："我没有做过 DPO，这块我需要学习。"
✅ 正确框架（3步）：
1. 展示你理解机制："DPO collapses RLHF's three steps into a single contrastive loss over (prompt, chosen, rejected) triples, optimizing the policy to increase the relative probability of chosen over rejected responses. β controls the KL divergence constraint."

2. 用 Reflexion 类比："In production, I implemented Reflexion — conceptually related: the model generates, critiques against criteria, and regenerates. It's a form of online preference-driven self-correction. DPO is the offline formalization of the same idea."

3. 说明快速上手："I've read the DPO and IPO papers, I know the TRL implementation, and I have a specific plan to run a small experiment in the next week. Given my SFT experience, the ramp-up will be fast."
